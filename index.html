Heavy Equipment Operator

As a Heavy Equipment Operator I was able to learn how to multi-task and provide customer service.
Tasks, under this job title, inlcuded: prepping sites for foundations, digging ditches for drain tile, digging craters for lakes, and maintaining heavy equipment.
I used backhoes, skid steers, trackhoes, trenchers, front-end buckets, shovels, GPS equipment, trucks, and other various tools to perform job related tasks.
This job was physically exhausting at times but very rewarding when clients would send you referrals for new jobs.

Lab Technician

Being a Lab Technician helped me learn communication skills and furthered along my customer service skills.
Under this job title I would sometimes draw patient's blood or retrieve other kinds of specimens. Since this required making relationships with people, I learned how to deal with people who were experiencing a lot of stress.
I would also send in specimens to the main lab for testing to be performed. Once the test/s were finalized we would let the patient's doctor know of the outcome.
This job position was rewarding because I was able to help people just by being there for them and having a listening ear.

Piano Technician

With this being my current job I can talk a lot about it. It is a very unique job position to have.
Sometimes I call myself a piano surgeon. Most of the work I perform includes restoring and repairing older pianos.
I usually have jobs scheduled in advance up to a few weeks. Therefore, I am able to look ahead of what jobs I will be taking on so I know what to expect.
I tune pianos, move/deliver pianos, repair various parts and pieces to pianos, rebuild old or worn out pianos, and deal with any client issues.
This job has been my longest held job and since I have played piano since I was 10 this job came natural to me. I has helped me develop a lot of positive workings skills that can help me for the job I aspire to have in the near future.
